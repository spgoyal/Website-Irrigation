Tehri Dam Project: River Bhagirathi with its source at Gaumukh (Gangotri) after joining Alaknanda in Deoprayag becomes the Ganga. The Ganga after joining Bhilganga become the mighty river Ganga whose water is to tapped by Tehri Dam after 1.5 Km downstream of Sangam. The excess rain water would be tapped for production of Hydro power/Irrigation/Flood control/Fisheries/Tourism purposes- Hence called the Multipurpose Hydro Electric Project. Lakhwar Vyasi Project: This multipurpose project is situated about 72 Km from the district head-quater. The proposed construction : A concrete dam 204 Metre high near village lakhwar & 300 Megawatt underground powerhouse. A concrete dam 80 Metres high about 5 Km downstream of Lakhwar Dam in village Vyasi. With a Water flow in HRT (7 metre diameter X 2.7 Km length) a power is proposed with a capacity of 120 megawatt. To balance the effect of above structures a barrage would be constructed near vill Hathiyari about 3 Km downstream. With the completion of this project additional 9270 Lac extra electrical energy would suplemented to the existing grid of the state and 40000 Hectares Land would be irrigated Kishau Dam Project : Proposed abou 95 km from Dehradun on Tons river, one of the tributeries of Yamuna. A concrete gravity dam about 236 meters. Total extra electric power generated will be to the tune 1688.19 x 106. On completion of the project 1.015 maf irrigation water will be available, out of this only 0.515 maf will utilized Eastern Yamuna Canal to irrigate 97076 Hectares of extra land and the rest. 500 maf will be made available for drinking water to Delhi State. The estimated cost as on 1996 estimate was 34.55 crores. Sharda Canal system : The Sharda canal was constructed about 60 years ago to projective irrigate the district between Ganga & Ghaghra basin namely Pilibhit, Kheri, sitapur, Shahjhanpur, Hardoi, Unnao, Barabanki, Rae Barielly, Faizabad, Pratapgarh, Sultanpur, Jaunpur, Azamgarh, Ghazipur, Allahabad, & Varanasi . The area earmarked is 20 lac hectares cultivable land, but the capacity used is only 8.82 Lac being about 44.10 % of cultivable land. The upper reaches of canal irrigates about 42 % & the lower reaches irrigates only 19 % of cultivable land, one of the reason being availablity of water in the lower reaches. To make more water available in the central & Eastern districts of Sitapur, Lucknow, Barabanki, Faizabad, Rae Barielly, Pratapgarh, Allahabad, Sultanpur, Jaunpur, Azamgarh, Mau, Varanasi, Ballia and Ghazipur inter basin water resources of Ghagra river is proposed to be tapped. Modernization, concrete lining, construction of Ggaghra, Tanda, Dohrighat and Dalmau Pump Canal. Rajghat Dam Project: This dam in Bundelkhand region on river Betwa takes care of Irrigation facilities of Both Uttar Pradesh & Madhya Pradesh State. The financial burden will be borne by both states under the central command of Betwa River Board. This project has been cleared by Central Government. The canal network is proposed to be borne by individual state governments. The Pump canal system of Betwa, Gurshahai and Bargaon is being considered. Modhu Dam Poject : This dam in extremely backward district of Hamirpur in Bundelkhand region. 95 % of construction has been completed in Rath Tehsil on Birma River. History+
.
1823 First Irrigation Office opened at Saharanpur.
1830 Remodeling of old Moghul Canal on left bank of Yamuna taken up and commissioned as Eastern Yamuna Canal.
1840 Construction of Ganga Canal started and completed in 1854.
1874 Commissioning of Agra Canal.
1878 Commissioning of Lower Ganga Canal.
1879 Unprecedented famine took heavy toll of life in Bundelkhand.
1880 First Famine Commission set up.
Commission recommended that 40% of the cropped area should be provided with irrigation facilities.
Central Public Works Department Established
1885 Betwa Canal opened to Irrigate Jalaun and Hamirpur districts.
1903 Second Famine Commission setup.
Commission reiterated the provision of Irrigation facilities as above.
1913 Hydro Power Station on Upper Ganga Canal started at Bahadrabad (Near Hardwar).
1930 Tube well construction commended in Meerut District.
1939 Steam Power Station at Sohawal in District Faizabad to supply Power for Pump Canal on River Ghaghra.
1939 Separate Hydro Electric wing named "Hydel" was set up in the Department.
1950 Hydro Electric wing seperated, Electricity Department was set up
1945 After IInd world war, emphasis on grow more food schemes. Investigation of new projects. Commercial policy underwent radical change at independence emphasis was on welfare of presentoverall deployment of State.
1949 Research Institute at Roorkee was formed.
1954 First flood work entrusted to Irrigation Deptartment.
1961 Rihand Dam completed; Project cost Rs 28 crores.
1961 On completition of second five year plan, following major measures were taken-
  • Setting up of central Design Directorate at Lucknow.
  • Central Organisation at Lucknow for procurement of Store & Equipment at Lucknow.
  • Creation of Post of Chief Engineer for each major project.
  • Creation of Post of Engineer-in-Chief.
  • Creation of Post of Liasion Officer at New Delhi for dealing import licenses and foreign exchange.
1964 Technical Audit Cell for Irrigation at Secretariat was set up.
1965 Separate Organisation for looking after construction and operation of State Tube-Wells under Director of Tube-Wells was set up.
1966 During severe drought in 1966, existing system of Irrigation was found inadequate, and a need was felt for lift irrigation. Lift irrigation schemes were take up to fight the unprecedented drought conditions.
1968 Directorate of Lift Irrigation was created.
